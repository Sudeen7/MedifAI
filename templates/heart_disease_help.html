Understanding Heart Disease Prediction Attributes

This page explains the attributes used in predicting heart disease and their significance.

1. Age

Description: Age of the individual in years.

Significance: The risk of heart disease increases with age.

2. Sex

Description: Biological sex (0 = Female, 1 = Male).

Significance: Men are generally at higher risk for heart disease, though postmenopausal women also face increased risk.

3. Chest Pain Type

Description: Type of chest pain experienced:

  • 0: Typical Angina (related to reduced blood flow to the heart)
  • 1: Atypical Angina (not related to the heart)
  • 2: Non-anginal Pain (unrelated to heart issues)
  • 3: Asymptomatic (no chest pain)

Significance: Indicates the nature and potential cause of chest pain.

4. Resting Blood Pressure

Description: Blood pressure measured while the individual is at rest (in mmHg).

Significance: High resting blood pressure (hypertension) increases the risk of heart disease.

5. Serum Cholesterol

Description: Level of cholesterol in the blood (mg/dL).

Significance: High cholesterol can lead to plaque buildup in arteries, increasing the risk of heart disease.

6. Fasting Blood Sugar

Description: Whether fasting blood sugar is >120 mg/dL (1 = Yes, 0 = No).

Significance: High fasting blood sugar is an indicator of diabetes, a major risk factor for heart disease.

7. Resting ECG Results

Description: Resting electrocardiographic results:

  • 0: Normal
  • 1: ST-T wave abnormality (e.g., T wave inversions, ST elevation or depression)
  • 2: Left Ventricular Hypertrophy (enlarged heart's main chamber)

Significance: Helps identify electrical activity and structural abnormalities of the heart.

8. Maximum Heart Rate Achieved

Description: The highest heart rate achieved during exercise or stress testing.

Significance: Indicates the heart's capacity to handle physical activity.

9. Exercise-Induced Angina

Description: Whether angina occurs during exercise (1 = Yes, 0 = No).

Significance: Suggests whether the heart receives enough blood during physical activity.

10. Oldpeak

Description: ST depression induced by exercise relative to rest.

Significance: Indicates ischemia (reduced blood flow to the heart).

11. Slope of the Peak Exercise ST Segment

Description: The slope of the ST segment during peak exercise:

  • 0: Upsloping
  • 1: Flat
  • 2: Downsloping

Significance: Reflects the heart's response to stress and exercise.

12. Number of Major Vessels

Description: Number of major blood vessels (0-3) visible through fluoroscopy.

Significance: A higher number of affected vessels indicates more severe coronary artery disease.

13. Thalassemia (Thal)

Description: Thalassemia status:

  • 0: Normal
  • 1: Fixed Defect (permanent heart damage)
  • 2: Reversible Defect (damage that can improve)

Significance: Helps assess the type and extent of heart-related damage.
