Diabetes Prediction - Understanding the Attributes

The diabetes prediction model considers various medical and demographic factors to assess the likelihood of diabetes. Below is a detailed explanation of each attribute used in the model:

  • Pregnancies:

    The number of times the individual has been pregnant. This helps in understanding reproductive health factors which can influence diabetes risk.

  • Glucose:

    The plasma glucose concentration (mg/dL) from an oral glucose tolerance test. Elevated glucose levels are a key indicator of diabetes or pre-diabetes.

  • BloodPressure:

    Measured diastolic blood pressure (mm Hg). High blood pressure is often associated with diabetes and other metabolic disorders.

  • SkinThickness:

    Thickness of the triceps skin fold (in mm). This serves as a proxy for body fat percentage, which is linked to diabetes risk.

  • Insulin:

    The serum insulin level (μU/mL). Low or high levels can indicate insulin resistance, a hallmark of diabetes.

  • BMI:

    Body Mass Index calculated as weight in kilograms divided by the square of height in meters (kg/m²). A BMI above normal levels increases the risk of diabetes.

  • DiabetesPedigreeFunction:

    A score indicating the genetic predisposition to diabetes, based on family history. A higher score indicates a greater likelihood of diabetes.

  • Age:

    The age of the individual in years. Age is a crucial factor as the risk of diabetes increases with age, especially after 45 years.

Note: These attributes collectively provide a comprehensive profile for the prediction of diabetes. While the model aids in identifying risk, it is not a substitute for a professional medical diagnosis.
